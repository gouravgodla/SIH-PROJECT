Ashwagandha

  • Synonym:
    असगंध (Asgandh) नागौरी (Nagauri) सोनाक्षी (Sonakshi) बाजीगर (Bajigar)
  • Plant Description:
    Type: A small, perennial shrub.
    Height: Grows between 35-75 cm (14-30 inches). Habitat: Prefers dry regions and is commonly found in India, the Middle East, and parts of Africa.
  • Root:
    Description: The root is thick, fleshy, and tuberous, typically used in powdered form.
    Odor: Has a horse-like smell, which is why it's called "Ashwagandha" (smell of horse).
    Uses: The root is the most important medicinal part of the plant, used for various therapeutic applications like stress relief, vitality, and immunity enhancement.
  • Stem:
    Structure: Woody and branching with a light brown hue.
    Function: Provides the plant with its structure, though it’s not commonly used in medicine.
  • Leaf:
    Shape: Simple, ovate (egg-shaped), and slightly pubescent (hairy).
    Size: Approximately 5-10 cm long. Uses: Sometimes used in poultices to reduce inflammation and pain.
  • Flower:
    Color: Small and greenish-yellow. Structure: The flowers are bisexual and borne in clusters.
    Uses: Not commonly used in medicinal formulations.
  • Fruit:
    Shape: Small, red berries (round).
    Size: About the size of a pea.
    Uses: The fruit is not typically used for medicinal purposes but contains beneficial compounds.
  • Parts Used: Primarily the root, but sometimes leaves and berries are also used in specific formulations.
  • Dosage (Traditional Ayurvedic Usage): Root Powder (Churna): 3-6 grams daily. Capsules/Tablets: 250-500 mg twice daily.
    Extracts: Generally standardized to contain 5% withanolides (active compound), dosage can vary from 300-600 mg daily. Always consult an Ayurvedic doctor for specific dosages based on the individual's body type and health condition.
  • Ayurvedic Pharmacology:
    Rasa (Taste): Tikta (bitter), Kashaya (astringent).
    Guna (Qualities): Laghu (light), Snigdha (oily).
    Veerya (Potency): Ushna (hot).
    Vipaka (Post-digestive effect): Madhura (sweet).
    Dosha Action: Balances Vata and Kapha doshas.
  • Karma (Actions in Ayurveda):
    Rasayana (Rejuvenative): Promotes vitality and longevity.
    Balya (Strength-giving): Enhances strength and stamina.
    Vatahara (Pacifies Vata): Calms nervous disorders and reduces anxiety.
    Shothahara (Anti-inflammatory): Reduces swelling and inflammation.
    Shukrala (Enhances reproductive health): Supports sexual wellness and fertility.
